P21: Development of a Testing Setup to Assess Coating Adherence
of a New Liquid Biopsy Device

Abstract

This project develops a biomechanical testing platform to evaluate the coating adherence of a new liquid biopsy device under realistic clinical conditions. A physiologically accurate phantom pelvis—comprising synthetic uterus, cervix, and vagina—is fabricated via multi-material 3D bioprinting using elastomeric and hydrogel polymers to replicate the surface texture and compliance of biological tissue. MRI segmentation (ITK-Snap / 3D Slicer) is used to derive anatomically faithful organ geometry from DICOM imaging data. The setup measures normal force, torque, and friction coefficient across repeated insertion cycles, with a repeatability target of CV ≤ 10%, enabling systematic, in-vitro screening of device coatings prior to clinical validation.
